Gear Extrusion: Setup

Description: This simulation shows the formation of gear teeth using the extrusion process. On the left, the setup consists of a green extrusion die and a yellow punch. As the punch pushes the red meshed billet into the die, the embossed teeth are engraved. On the right, the transition is shown from initial billet (top), to extruded billet (middle), and final machined gear (bottom).

Gear Extrusion: Strain Analysis

Description: This analysis focuses on material flow during the extrusion process. As the yellow punch drives the red billet through the green die, different strain contours are visible on the right. These values can be interpreted using the color scale provided on the left of the screen.

Gear Extrusion: Force vs. Displacement

Description: On the right-hand side, a graph represents the Force on Punch versus Pilot Height. Pilot Height (mm) is plotted on the X-axis, while Force (Tonnes) is on the Y-axis, quantifying the mechanical energy required for successful gear extrusion.

Bevel Gear: Forging Step 1

Description: This simulation shows the formation of straight bevel gears. Step 1 demonstrates the deformation of the initial billet into an intermediate phase using a downward-moving yellow die and a stationary green die. The right side displays the resulting deformed billet profile.

Bevel Gear: Forging Step 2

Description: The intermediate billet from Step 1 is placed in the Step 2 dies. The upper yellow die forces the material into the required final bevel gear shape against the stationary green base, completing the tooth formation.

Bevel Gear: Strain and Force Analysis

Description: This simulation analyzes Step 1 forging. The top-right shows equivalent strain generation (average 0.61) as the die descends. The forging force graph (bottom right) shows a peak of 10.2 Tonnes. The left panel shows the physical simulation of the metal flow.
